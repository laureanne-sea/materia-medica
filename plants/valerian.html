Valerian

Botanical Name

Valeriana officinalis

Body Systems Affected

Nervous System

Common Names

English: Valerian, Garden heliotrope

Danish: Baldrian, Læge-baldrian

French: Valériane officinale

Habitat and Growing Conditions

Sunlight: Prefers full sun but can tolerate partial shade. Too much shade can lead to floppy stems.

Soil: Prefers rich, heavy loam with plenty of moisture. It thrives in well-draining soil and can adapt to less ideal conditions. Avoid highly acidic soils.

Moisture: Needs consistently moist conditions, but should not be waterlogged. Some varieties, like Red Valerian, can tolerate dry periods once established.

Climate: grows well in both hot and cold climates and prefers cooler summers, so it may need afternoon shade in hotter, southern regions.

Soil pH: a range of pH levels.

Maintenance: prevent self-seeding, cut off faded flowers before seeds form.

Considerations: aware that valerian can be invasive in some areas, so check local regulations before planting

Active Constituents

Essential oil (mainly sesquiterpenes)

Iridoids also known as "valepotriates"

Flavonoids

Lignans

Baldrinals in the dried root resulting from the degradation of valepotriates

Properties

Sedative, antispasmodic, hypotensive (blood pressure lowering), sleep-inducing, carminative, nerve tonic, analgesic (pain-relieving), stimulating, mucus expelling, diuretic

Side Effects

In case of overdose, headache, heaviness and feverish drowsiness may occur.

Pertinent Information

The root mass is very small compared to its aerial mass.

Family

Valerianaceae, Perennial plant

Parts Used

Root

Forms Used and Doses

Dried in infusion (note: a root generally requires a decoction, but given the aromatic nature of valerian root, it is better to make an infusion), or fresh or dried in tincture. The tincture of fresh or "nearly fresh" roots with the highest possible alcohol content is vastly superior to the tincture of dried roots.

To be effective, Valerian needs to be taken in sufficiently high doses, 4 cups of tea daily or a teaspoon of tincture taken several times daily, or as needed
